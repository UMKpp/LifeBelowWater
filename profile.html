Sylvia A. Earle

Marine Biologist · Oceanographer · Explorer · "Her Deepness"

"No ocean, no life. No blue, no green. No ocean, no us."

Oceanographer National Geographic Explorer TED Prize 2009 Time Hero for the Planet

Born — August 30, 1935

Age — 90

Occupation — Marine Biologist & Oceanographer

Location — Oakland, CA

Skills

Goals

  • Protect 30% of the world's oceans by 2030 (30×30 movement)
  • Expand Mission Blue's Hope Spots to every critical ocean zone
  • Establish ecocide as a crime at the International Criminal Court
  • Advance public understanding of ocean biodiversity loss
  • Inspire the next generation of ocean scientists and conservationists

Frustrations

  • ▶90% depletion of large fish populations due to commercial fishing
  • ▶Slow political action on marine protection and ocean policy
  • ▶Factory fishing "vacuuming up" marine ecosystems
  • ▶Insufficient global funding for deep ocean exploration

Experience

First Chief Scientist of NOAA (1990–1992) — first woman in the role
National Geographic Explorer at Large since 1998
Set women's solo dive depth record of 381 m (1,250 ft) in 1979 — still unbroken
Led first all-female aquanaut team in Tektite II (1970)
Founded Deep Ocean Engineering (1982) & DOER Marine (1992)
Authored 150+ scientific publications and 14+ books
PhD in Phycology, Duke University (1966)
Research fellow at Harvard University (1967–1981)
